Moderate Info Consumption

We live in an "attention economy". This means that businesses are not only completing for what's in your wallet, but they're also competing for your attention. And they do so by constantly serving you new content (e.g., more news articles and new TV series).

What's worse is that our brains love novelty, so new ideas are some times more attractive to us than familiar ideas. For instance, a YouTube video you have never yet watched will appear more attractive than one you have already watched. As a consequence, you might find yourself consuming more information than you can mentally digest and make useful.

The truth is that you and I have already been exposed to some extremely useful ideas. What we really need to do is stick to those ideas, re-expose ourselves to them, and execute. Drive your life with the good ideas that you're already familiar with.

Moreover, it's useful to moderate the amount of new ideas you consume. It really doesn't hurt to consume less.
